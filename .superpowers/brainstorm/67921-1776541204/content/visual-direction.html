What visual direction do you want?

"Better graphics" can mean different things. Which of these vibes fits what you're going for?

A

PS1-Authentic Horror

Think: Silent Hill 1, Resident Evil 1

Low-res textures (64x64, 128x128) on everything. Affine texture warping. Vertex jitter/wobble. Dithered transparency. CRT post-processing (scanlines, vignette, chromatic aberration). The PS1 couldn't render cleanly — lean into that.

Adds: Procedural textures on all surfaces, vertex jitter shader, CRT post-processing, film grain, dithered fog
Keeps: Half-res render, flat shading, no antialiasing
Vibe: Nostalgic, uncanny, "something is wrong with the world itself"
B

Modern Lo-Fi Horror

Think: Puppet Combo, Iron Lung, Paratopic

Textured surfaces but deliberately degraded. No vertex jitter. Pixelated but with better lighting (emissive glow, fog gradients). VHS-style post-processing instead of CRT. Keeps the lo-fi feel but doesn't try to be a PS1.

Adds: Procedural textures, emissive materials, VHS post-processing (noise, tracking lines), improved fog
Keeps: Half-res render, pixelated upscale, flat shading
Vibe: Found-footage, oppressive, "you shouldn't be watching this"
C

Textured + Clean (No Retro Filter)

Think: Better Minecraft with horror lighting

Add textures and better materials to the existing geometry, but skip retro post-processing. Full resolution render. Smooth shading where it matters. Focus on atmosphere through lighting and fog rather than visual degradation.

Adds: Procedural textures, Phong materials with specularity, full-res render, better geometry
Removes: Half-res pixelation, flat shading
Vibe: Modern indie horror, atmospheric, "this basement is real"
