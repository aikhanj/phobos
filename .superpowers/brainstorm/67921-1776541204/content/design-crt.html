Design Section 3: CRT Post-Processing

Full-screen shader pass that transforms the clean render into a degraded CRT signal

How It Works

After Three.js renders the scene to the half-res buffer, an EffectComposer runs a single custom ShaderPass that applies all CRT effects in one fragment shader. This is cheap — it's just a full-screen quad with texture reads.

New file: apps/web/src/game/postProcessing.ts
Exports a createCRTComposer(renderer, scene, camera) function. The engine calls composer.render() instead of renderer.render().

Effects Included

Scanlines

Alternating dark horizontal lines every 2-3 pixels. Opacity modulated by a sine wave tied to screen Y. Subtle enough to not obscure gameplay but visible enough to read as "CRT."

Vignette

Darkened edges — radial falloff from center. Focuses the player's eye on the center of the screen and hides edge geometry. Classic horror framing.

Chromatic Aberration

R/G/B channels sampled at slightly offset UVs, splitting at the edges. Simulates a cheap CRT tube with imperfect electron beam convergence. Intensifies during scare events.

Film Grain

Per-frame random noise overlay. Low opacity (~0.06) but adds analog texture to every pixel. Driven by a time uniform so it's never static. Can intensify with fear_score.

Horror Integration

The CRT shader exposes uniforms that the scare director can drive:

  • uAberrationStrength — ramps up during scares (0.002 → 0.01)
  • uGrainIntensity — increases with fear_score
  • uScanlineWeight — can pulse heavier for TV-static moments
  • uVignetteRadius — tightens during high tension to create tunnel vision

These tie directly into the existing SceneConfig system and the agent tick loop.
